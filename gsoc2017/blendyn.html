Blendyn, the post-processor for MBDyn

I contributed to MBDyn this summer, as a part of my project in Google Summer of Code, 2017, under the mentorship of Louis Gagnon, and Andrea Zanoni.

Overview

MBDyn is the first and possibly the only free general purpose Multibody Dynamics analysis software.

It has been developed at the Dipartimento di Scienze e Tecnologie Aerospaziali (formerly Dipartimento di Ingegneria Aerospaziale) of the University “Politecnico di Milano”, Italy.

The results of an MBDyn simulation are in either text form, or in binary (specifically, netCDF format). Users can use these results files, and plot data with their tool of choice(Matplotlib, MATLAB, etc).

But plotting linear coordinates/Euler angles on a 2D plot, even though highly customizable, does not provide a great visual feedback.

This is the problem that Blendyn aims to solve.

Blendyn is a bridge between the animation capabilities of Blender, the most popular open source animation software, and data from MBDyn results.

In Blendyn, you can import results files generated by MBDyn, animate them, and render videos from these animations.

During the last couple of months, I’ve been working to add some much needed features to Blendyn, such as:

  • Running MBDyn from Blendyn.
  • Automatic visualization of a bunch of MBDyn joint elements, such as inline, inplane, axial rotation, gimbal, etc.
  • Automatic visualization of deformable joints.
  • Text overlay of MBDyn variables in Blender renders, and some new features to plotting within Blendyn.
  • Sync MBDyn execution and the Blender animation in order to see the Blender scene evolve during the actual MBDyn simulation.

amongst others.
